I am a technology executive and enterprise architect with a track record of translating complex organizational strategy into scalable, high-impact technology platforms. My career spans Fortune 50 automotive OEMs, global manufacturing, and financial services — where I have consistently led architecture at the intersection of emerging technology and enterprise reality.

At Ford Motor Company, I served as a standing member of the Autonomous Vehicle Technical Council and led enterprise architecture initiatives spanning connected vehicles, UBI frameworks deployed across 26 countries, and AV platform strategy. At DaimlerChrysler/FCA/Stellantis, I led post-merger application portfolio rationalization through structured architecture governance, and architected the Global Bill of Materials for Mopar Strategic Services.

Across every engagement, my focus has been the same: build architecture that endures, enables business agility, and delivers measurable outcomes. I bring deep expertise in TOGAF-based EA governance, SOA/BIAN transformation, AI-driven integration platforms, and digital transformation at enterprise scale.

What distinguishes my approach is the ability to operate at multiple altitudes — from enterprise strategy and governance to hands-on architecture design — and to translate that thinking across industries as different as automotive OEM, financial services, and industrial manufacturing.

Throughout my career I have led and mentored cross-functional teams of architects and engineers, guiding both technical direction and individual professional growth across complex enterprise programs.

I hold 13 granted patents (12 U.S., 1 German) spanning connected vehicles, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity — and I publish regularly on technology strategy and the future of mobility.
